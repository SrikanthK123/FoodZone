        <p>Soak Basmati Rice:

Wash basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
Boil Rice:

In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
Add salt, whole spices, and soaked basmati rice.
Cook the rice until it is 70-80% cooked. It should still have a slight bite to it.
Drain the water and set aside.
Biryani Masala:
Sauté Onions:

In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee.
Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add Aromatics:

Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
Add green chilies and chopped tomatoes. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
Spices:

Add biryani masala powder, red chili powder, and turmeric powder. Mix well.
Stir in yogurt, fresh coriander leaves, and mint leaves.
Cooking Meat or Vegetables:

If making chicken or mutton biryani, add marinated and partially cooked meat to the masala.
For vegetable biryani, add mixed vegetables.
Simmer:

Allow the meat or vegetables to cook in the masala until they are almost done.
Layering:
Layering Rice and Masala:

In a heavy-bottomed pot or biryani handi, start layering.
Begin with a layer of partially cooked rice.
Add a layer of the meat or vegetable masala.
Repeat the layers until all the rice and masala are used.
Fried Onions and Ghee:

Top the biryani with fried onions and drizzle ghee or cooking oil.
Optionally, add saffron-soaked milk for color.
Dum Cooking:

Seal the pot with a tight-fitting lid or aluminum foil to create a dum.
Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
Fluffing and Serving:

Gently fluff the biryani with a fork.
Serve hot, garnished with fresh coriander and mint leaves.
Enjoy your flavorful biryani! Adjust the spice levels and ingredients according to your taste preferences.</p>
